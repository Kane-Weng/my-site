<h2 class="section-header build">Overview</h2>
The **SIM-26** project is a large-scale multidisciplinary research initiative at the University of Michigan aimed at studying driver workload and distraction. As the Technical Lead and Software Engineer for the "Wizard of Oz" subteam, I oversee the development of a high-fidelity, multiplayer simulation environment in **Unreal Engine 5**. Our goal is to create a driving simulation realistic enough that the human physiological data collected is valid for real-world application.

<h2 class="section-header teach">Engineering Leadership in a 20-Person Team</h2>
Leading a 4-person subteam in a large research environment has fundamentally changed my approach to software development. With 20 researchers working on different aspects of the simulation, communication is the primary technical challenge.

To prevent the system from crashing during integration, I’ve implemented two key leadership strategies in my subteam:
* **Pre-emptive Reporting:** We enforce weekly subteam reports before the general assembly to flag architectural changes before they affect other teams.
* **Documentation as Scalability:** I’ve shifted my focus from just writing code to building robust **code structures and documentation**. In a team this size, good documentation is a force multiplier, as it saves me from repeating technical explanations and allows others to build on my work directly.

<h2 class="section-header learn">Technical Deep Dive: UE5 Multiplayer & Spawning</h2>
My current focus is architecting the **multiplayer server infrastructure**. This involves bridging the gap between high-level UE5 Blueprints and underlying C++ logic to ensure a seamless experience for multiple research participants.

A significant technical hurdle occurred while developing the vehicle spawning logic for late-joining players. The level map consistently crash the client without throwing a standard console error. 
* **The Culprit:** Unreal’s collision handling for overlapping actors. Spawning multiple vehicles at the same global coordinate caused a physics conflict that crashed the game state.
* **The Fix:** I developed a dynamic spawning algorithm that recomputes safe coordinates for new players, ensuring the server remains stable regardless of participant count.
